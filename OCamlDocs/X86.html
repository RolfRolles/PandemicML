Module X86


module X86: sig .. end
Generic description of x86/32 assembly language. Current as of the XYZ revision of the Intel manuals.

Each instruction is represented by: a list of its X86.x86_group1_prefixes, a mnemonic (its X86.x86mnem), and a list of its operands (X86.x86operands).

The enumeration for mnemonics is just a giant collection of nullary sum type constructors: X86.x86mnem.

Each operand is one of the following:

  • A general register from the X86.x86_general_reg enumeration, whose type is further broken down into
    • Gb(r), where r is Al, Cl, ... from the X86.x86_reg8 enumeration.
    • Gw(r), where r is Ax, Cx, ... from the X86.x86_reg16 enumeration.
    • Gd(r}, where r is Eax, Ecx, ... from the X86.x86_reg32 enumeration.
  • A control register, e.g. CR0, from the X86.x86_control_reg enumeration.
  • A debug register, e.g. DR0, from the X86.x86_debug_reg enumeration.
  • A segment register, e.g. DS, from the X86.x86_segreg enumeration.
  • An FPU register, e.g. ST0, from the X86.x86_fpureg enumeration.
  • An MMX register, e.g. MM0, from the X86.x86_mmxreg enumeration.
  • An XMM register, e.g. XMM0, from the X86.x86_xmmreg enumeration.
  • A immediate value, whose type is further broken down into
    • Ib(i), where i is an 8-bit value in an int32
    • Iw(i), where i is a 16-bit value in an int32
    • Id(i}, where i is a 32-bit value in an int32
  • A memory expression from the X86.x86_mem_expr enumeration, whose type is further broken down into
    • Mb (m)
    • Mw (m)
    • Md (m}
    • Mf (m)
    • Mq (m)
    • Mt (m}
    • Mdq(m}
    where m is from the X86.x86_addr_expr enumeration:
    • Mem16(seg,basereg option,scalereg option,displacement option)
    • Mem32(seg,basereg option,(scalereg * scalefactor) option,displacement option)
  • A jump target, which is a pair of int32s
  • A 32:16 (Ap16) or 32:32 (Ap32) far address, from the X86.x86_far_target enumeration
